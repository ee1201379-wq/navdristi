Feature Modules

The new capabilities added to the platform.

01

Voice-Based Complaints

Citizens can report issues in local languages through voice input, reducing literacy and typing barriers during stressful situations.

02

Emotion Detection

Detects anger, fear, and distress from complaint content or voice cues so urgent public-risk cases move up the queue immediately.

03

Predictive Issue Detection

Looks at complaint trends, ward history, and seasonal patterns to predict recurring issues before they escalate city-wide.

04

Live Priority Heatmap

Displays complaints from high to low priority across wards so administrators instantly see which areas need action now.

05

Auto-Assignment Engine

Routes each complaint to the right department and suggests likely resolutions based on similar historic cases.

06

Delay-Based Auto-Escalation

If a case remains unresolved beyond SLA windows, the system escalates it automatically to the next authority layer.

07

Image Verification

Checks uploaded evidence for manipulation, mismatch, or reused media to reduce fake or misleading complaint submissions.

08

Community Voting System

Residents can support complaints in their area so the most visible civic issues rise faster for officials to review.

09

Real-Time AI Talk

Includes a voice assistant that listens, answers, and speaks back like Alexa so citizens can ask for guidance without typing.

10

Audio Evidence Recording

Lets citizens record and attach original audio clips so teams can review the raw voice complaint alongside transcripts and images.

Processing Flow

How the AI pipeline works.

1

Intake in any language

Voice or typed complaints are normalized into structured issue reports with translated summaries where needed.

2

Urgency and authenticity checks

Emotion signals, severity cues, duplicate matches, and image verification shape the first response priority.

3

Department routing and fix suggestions

The engine recommends who should own the case and what likely resolution path can shorten closure time.

4

Monitoring and escalation

Live heatmaps and SLA timers keep the case visible, while unresolved delays trigger automatic escalation.

Impact Story

Why these features matter in the demo.

Better accessibility

Voice-based reporting helps more citizens participate, especially in fast-moving or low-literacy scenarios.

Stronger urgency handling

Emotion-aware prioritization helps teams respond sooner to potentially dangerous complaints.

Operational foresight

Predicted issue spikes help city teams prevent floods, outages, sanitation overflows, and traffic failures before escalation.

Higher trust

Image verification and transparent escalation make the platform harder to misuse and easier to defend in a hackathon pitch.
